# Founders at work

**Q. Out of 32 interviews, how many are technical person?**


In Founders at Work, roughly 26–28 out of the 32 interviewees were strongly technical founders — programmers, engineers, or people deeply involved in building the product themselves.

Examples include:

- Steve Wozniak (Ch. 3)
- Paul Graham (Ch. 15)
- David Heinemeier Hansson (Ch. 23)
- Max Levchin (Ch. 1)
- Joel Spolsky (Ch. 25)

Only a small minority were primarily business/investment-oriented rather than hands-on technical builders.

**Q. Who are non-technical?**

The clearly non-technical or less-technical people in Founders at Work were roughly:

- Ann Winblad — more business/investing oriented.
- Tim Brady — business/operations side rather than engineering.
- Caterina Fake — product/community/design oriented, not mainly technical.
- James Currier — more product/business focused.
- Bob Davis — business/media side.
- Possibly Craig Newmark depending on definition — he did technical work early on, but is not viewed as a hardcore engineer in the same way as Wozniak or Levchin.

Almost everyone else in the book was strongly engineering/programming oriented. That’s one reason the book became so popular among developers and technical founders.
