# Naval on Artificial Intelligence

17 May 2026 (Source: [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com/c/6a098b57-9314-83ec-bf52-fbd686932e17))

Naval Ravikant generally talks about AI as a massive “leverage multiplier” rather than just a tool.

A few recurring ideas from him:

* AI will automate “clearly bounded” work — repetitive, rules-based tasks, even a lot of coding. ([Financial Express][1])
* Human advantage shifts toward:

  * judgment
  * taste
  * creativity
  * original thinking
  * entrepreneurship
* He believes AI makes “one-person companies” dramatically more powerful because a single person can now do work that once needed teams. ([Startup Archive][2])
* He’s more worried about centralized control of AI than AI itself:

  > “I’m not scared of AI. I’m scared of what a very small number of people who control AI do to the rest of us.” ([windinmyface.com][3])
* He’s skeptical of pure AI doom narratives and often argues that incentives and regulation can become bigger risks than the technology. ([windinmyface.com][3])
* On programmers: he’s said AI will heavily assist coding, but great programmers won’t disappear because defining the right problem and exercising judgment still matters. ([Financial Express][1])

One very “Naval-style” takeaway:

> In an AI world, execution becomes cheaper, so knowing *what to build* becomes more valuable than knowing only *how to build it*.
